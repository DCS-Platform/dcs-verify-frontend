Why this matters

When an AI agent acts, can you prove what happened?

AI agents now make decisions, call tools, access systems, generate content, approve workflows, and perform real business actions. Every organization running them needs to answer five questions — to a regulator, a customer, or a court.

Traditional logs

Answer only if you trust the operator

  • Can be edited or deleted after the fact
  • No way to prove they weren't altered
  • Disputable — it's your word against theirs
  • Auditors must trust your internal systems
DCS Verify receipts

Anyone can verify the evidence themselves

  • What happened — captured in a signed receipt
  • Who performed it — bound to the issuer's key
  • Has it been altered — tamper-evident hash chain
  • Independently auditable — anchored on-chain, public

Good questions

Before you apply.

Do the people verifying my receipts need an account?
No. Verification is public and free. Anyone with a receipt can re-check the signature, the hash chain, and the on-chain anchor themselves — without trusting DCS or contacting you.
What happens after the 60 days?
During beta everything is free. Paid plans come later, once the network has real issuers and real verification volume. Founding issuers get first access and preferential terms when pricing is introduced.
Am I locked into DCS?
No. Receipts are built on an open specification and are portable. The reference verifier is open — a third party can confirm your receipts independently of our servers.
What does it take to integrate?
You emit a receipt at the moment an action occurs. Signing, chaining, Filecoin storage, and Base anchoring happen automatically. Priority onboarding support is included for founding issuers.
